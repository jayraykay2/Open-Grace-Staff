Who We Are

Open Grace LLC is a Coordinated Family Services (CFS) provider operating under contract with Inland Counties Regional Center (IRC). We support individuals and families navigating the regional center system by providing direct coordination, service navigation, and advocacy to ensure each client receives the care and support they are entitled to.

Your Role & Responsibilities

CFS Staff — Core Responsibilities
  • Provide direct service coordination to assigned clients including referrals, appointments, and resource navigation under the guidance of your supervisor or director
  • Log all service contacts in the Open Grace CFS system within 24 hours of each interaction
  • Submit quarterly reports (QR) to IRC on time — by the 1st of the month following each 90-day interval
  • Maintain professional, consistent communication with clients, families, and IRC service coordinators
  • Monitor and manage authorized service hours — alert supervisor when approaching 80% of authorized limit
  • Attend all required staff meetings, trainings, and IRC compliance sessions
  • Maintain current CPR certification and Live Scan clearance at all times
  • Report all Special Incident Reports (SIRs) verbally within 24 hours and in writing within 48 hours
⚠️
Documentation Requirement All service contacts must be logged the same day or within 24 hours. Late or missing documentation affects IRC billing and quarterly reporting compliance.
🚨
Mandatory Reporting Any incident involving client safety, abuse, neglect, or unusual circumstances must be reported to your supervisor immediately and documented as a Special Incident Report (SIR). There are no exceptions.

Client Interaction Protocols

First Contact with a New Client
1

Review the client file

Before any contact, review the Client Roster entry, IPP, and CDER in the CFS system. Note diagnosis, communication method, safety flags, and authorized services.

2

Introduce Open Grace

Contact the family via phone within 5 business days of assignment. Introduce yourself by name, explain your role, and confirm the best way to reach them going forward.

3

Assessment is conducted by supervisor or director

The initial client assessment is completed by your supervisor or director — not the family coordinator. As direct support staff, you will be present to observe, take notes, and receive specific guidance and instruction on how to support that client going forward.

4

Receive your service instructions

Following the assessment, your supervisor or director will brief you on the client's service plan, goals, approved activities, and any safety or communication considerations. You carry out services as directed — never independently modifying the plan without supervisor approval.

5

Log the contact

Enter the contact in the Open Grace CFS system the same day — include date, contact type, hours, CFS component, and session notes. This starts the service record.

Ongoing Client Interaction Standards
  • Always identify yourself by name and agency at the start of every contact
  • Use clear, simple language — many clients and families may have limited English proficiency
  • For nonverbal clients, communicate directly through gestures, visuals, or family support as appropriate
  • Never share client information with anyone not listed on the authorized contacts list
  • Maintain professional boundaries at all times — do not accept gifts, share personal contact info, or engage in personal relationships with clients or families
  • If a client or family member expresses dissatisfaction, listen without defensiveness and escalate to supervisor
  • Always confirm the next appointment or contact date before ending each session
ℹ️
Confidentiality Reminder All client information is protected under Welfare & Institutions Code §4514. Never discuss client details in public, in shared digital spaces, or with unauthorized individuals. Violations carry serious legal consequences.
