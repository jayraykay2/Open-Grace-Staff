📋 CFS Component Guide
1
🏠 Living at Home
Identifying and providing supports to successfully reside in the family home
Use when you:
Helped family set up home routines Coordinated in-home support Addressed safety concerns at home Helped with daily living activities
2
🗺️ Finding Services & Resources
Navigating services and supports, creating pathways to overcoming barriers
Use when you:
Found a new program or benefit Helped with an application Removed a barrier to accessing services Linked to community resources
3
📋 Understanding the Diagnosis
Providing information or resources on the individual's diagnosis and identified supports
Use when you:
Explained diagnosis to family Shared educational materials Connected to a specialist or support group Discussed behavior strategies
4
🤝 Working with Other Providers
Coordinating consistency in training across providers
Use when you:
Contacted a school or therapist Attended an IEP or team meeting Shared info between providers Coordinated care approaches
5
📅 Appointments & Scheduling
Assisting with scheduling of service delivery including medical and other appointments
Use when you:
Scheduled a doctor visit Followed up on a missed appointment Set up therapy or evaluation Created a calendar or reminder system
6
🚗 Transportation
Identifying transportation options or services
Use when you:
Found a ride to appointments Applied for Dial-A-Ride or paratransit Discussed bus routes or Uber Health Arranged transport for activities
7
🛡️ Backup Support Plans
Identifying backup providers/supports when the plan fails
Use when you:
Created a backup care plan Found coverage when a provider cancelled Identified emergency contacts Planned for caregiver illness/absence
8
🔮 Future Planning
Providing futures planning, including those with aging caregivers
Use when you:
Discussed long-term living options Talked about what happens when caregivers age Helped with estate or guardianship planning Connected to transition planning resources
9
🎯 Building Independence
Providing training to the individual which maximizes their independence
Use when you:
Practiced daily living skills Worked on money management Taught self-advocacy Practiced communication or social skills
10
📝 Other
Need identified within scope of CFS not covered in #1–9
Use when the activity is clearly within CFS scope but doesn't fit neatly into any of the above. Always add a note explaining what it was.
💡 Tip: You can select more than one component per contact log entry. Most visits address 2–3 components. Your selections feed directly into the Quarterly Report (Enclosure D).
