1. WELCOME & MISSION STATEMENT

Welcome to Open Grace LLC.

Open Grace LLC is a Coordinated Family Support (CFS) provider dedicated to empowering individuals with developmental and intellectual disabilities and strengthening the families who support them. Our services are guided by dignity, compassion, professionalism, cultural responsiveness, and person-centered care.

As a vendorized provider through Inland Counties Regional Center (IRC), Open Grace LLC operates in accordance with applicable California Welfare and Institutions Code provisions, Title 17 California Code of Regulations, HIPAA requirements, and all applicable federal, state, and local regulations.

Our Mission

To provide individualized support that promotes independence, stability, safety, self-determination, and meaningful community inclusion for the individuals and families we serve.

Our Core Values

  • Support should adapt to the person, not require the person to adapt to the support.
  • Families deserve respect, partnership, and clear communication.
  • Professionalism and compassion must coexist.
  • Ethical and compliant service delivery protects both clients and staff.
  • Documentation, accountability, and integrity are essential to quality care.
  • Cultural responsiveness is not optional — it is a standard of practice.

2. AT-WILL EMPLOYMENT POLICY

Employment with Open Grace LLC is at-will. This means that either the employee or Open Grace LLC may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause, and with or without advance notice, subject to applicable California law.

Nothing in this handbook, any verbal statement, policy, practice, or procedure creates a contract of employment or alters the at-will nature of employment.

Only the Director of Open Grace LLC has the authority to enter into any agreement modifying at-will employment status, and any such agreement must be in writing and signed by both the employee and the Director.

4. FAMILY COORDINATOR JOB DESCRIPTION & EXPECTATIONS

Family Coordinators are the core of service delivery at Open Grace LLC. They provide direct Coordinated Family Support (CFS) services to consumers and families receiving services through Inland Counties Regional Center.

Primary Responsibilities

  • Provide direct support services to consumers and families in accordance with authorized service plans
  • Assist consumers with skill development, independent living goals, and community integration
  • Support linkage to community resources, programs, and natural supports
  • Maintain consistent communication with service coordinators and interdisciplinary teams
  • Complete accurate, objective, and timely documentation of all services rendered
  • Submit daily contact logs and CFS service forms as required by IRC
  • Report safety concerns, incidents, and suspected abuse immediately
  • Participate in required trainings, supervision, and team meetings
  • Maintain confidentiality and professional boundaries at all times

Performance Expectations

  • Punctuality and reliability in all scheduled shifts and client appointments
  • Professional communication with clients, families, and team members
  • Accurate and timely submission of all required documentation
  • Cultural responsiveness and person-centered approach in all interactions
  • Ethical conduct aligned with Open Grace LLC values and IRC standards
  • Completion of all required training within designated timeframes

5. WORK HOURS & SCHEDULING

Work schedules are assigned based on client needs, operational requirements, and program demands.

General Guidelines

  • Employees must arrive on time and prepared for all scheduled shifts and client visits
  • Accurate timesheets must be maintained and submitted on schedule
  • Overtime must be pre-authorized by the Director before hours are worked
  • Schedule changes require advance notice and Director approval
Falsification of service hours, timesheets, or documentation is strictly prohibited and will result in immediate termination and may be reported to appropriate licensing authorities.

6. CONFIDENTIALITY & HIPAA COMPLIANCE

Employees are required to maintain strict confidentiality regarding all consumer, family, employee, and organizational information. This obligation continues after employment ends.

Legal Obligations

  • HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules
  • Title 17 California Code of Regulations
  • Welfare and Institutions Code confidentiality provisions
  • Inland Counties Regional Center data sharing agreements

Prohibited Actions

  • Discussing confidential consumer information in public settings
  • Sharing protected health information with unauthorized individuals
  • Posting client-related information, photos, or identifying details online
  • Accessing consumer records beyond the scope of your assigned role
  • Removing or transmitting confidential documents outside authorized channels

Any suspected confidentiality breach must be reported immediately to the Director. Violations may result in disciplinary action, termination, and regulatory reporting.

7. CLIENT DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS (IRC COMPLIANCE)

Accurate documentation is a critical component of service delivery, regulatory compliance, and consumer protection.

Required Documentation

  • Daily Contact Logs — completed for every service interaction
  • CFS Service Forms — submitted per IRC schedule and format requirements
  • Incident Reports — filed immediately following any reportable incident
  • Progress Notes — aligned with consumer goals and authorized services
  • Service Acknowledgment Forms — signed by consumers/guardians as required

Documentation Standards

  • All documentation must be accurate, objective, legible, and professional
  • Documentation must be submitted within required timeframes
  • Entries must reflect services actually rendered — no pre-dating or falsification
  • Documentation must align with authorized services in the consumer's IPP
Failure to maintain accurate and timely documentation is a serious compliance issue and may result in disciplinary action, termination, and notification to IRC.

8. CODE OF CONDUCT & PROFESSIONAL STANDARDS

Expected Conduct

  • Treat all individuals with dignity, respect, and professionalism
  • Maintain appropriate professional boundaries with consumers and families
  • Avoid conflicts of interest and disclose any potential conflicts immediately
  • Follow all organizational policies, procedures, and directives
  • Cooperate fully with internal and external compliance reviews
  • Report concerns, safety issues, or misconduct promptly

Prohibited Conduct

The following conduct may result in disciplinary action up to and including immediate termination:

  • Dishonesty, fraud, or misrepresentation
  • Neglect or abandonment of assigned duties
  • Harassment, bullying, or discrimination of any kind
  • Confidentiality violations or unauthorized disclosure of protected information
  • Unsafe practices that endanger consumers, staff, or the public
  • Use, possession, or distribution of alcohol or controlled substances during work hours
  • Abuse, neglect, exploitation, or mistreatment of consumers
  • Theft or misappropriation of organizational or consumer property

9. MANDATED REPORTER OBLIGATIONS

All employees of Open Grace LLC are mandated reporters under California law. This is a non-negotiable legal obligation.

Reporting Requirements

  • Child abuse or neglect (Penal Code § 11164 et seq.)
  • Elder abuse or dependent adult abuse (Welfare & Institutions Code § 15600 et seq.)
  • Abuse, neglect, or exploitation of individuals receiving developmental services

How to Report

  1. Contact appropriate authorities immediately (APS, CPS, or law enforcement)
  2. Notify the Director of Open Grace LLC as soon as possible after reporting
  3. Complete any required internal incident documentation
Failure to report suspected abuse is a crime in California and may result in termination, civil liability, and criminal penalties.

10. SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY

Prohibited Online Conduct

  • Posting or sharing any confidential client information, photos, or identifying details
  • Discussing consumers, families, or colleagues in online forums or groups
  • Making derogatory statements about Open Grace LLC, IRC, or clients
  • Maintaining personal social media relationships with consumers or family members
  • Representing personal opinions as those of Open Grace LLC

Professional boundaries with consumers and families must be maintained across all social media platforms at all times, including after hours.

11. DRESS CODE & PROFESSIONAL APPEARANCE

Standards

  • Clothing should be clean, neat, and appropriate for a professional care setting
  • Closed-toe shoes are recommended during home visits for safety
  • Avoid excessive perfume or cologne, which may affect sensitive consumers

Prohibited Attire

  • Clothing with offensive language, graphics, or imagery
  • Excessively revealing, torn, or visibly damaged clothing
  • Clothing promoting drugs, alcohol, violence, or discrimination

12. TIME OFF, ABSENCES & CALL-OUT PROCEDURES

Call-Out Procedure

  1. Notify your supervisor as early as possible, no later than one hour before your scheduled shift
  2. Provide a reason for your absence and estimated return date if known
  3. Follow up with any required documentation (e.g., medical note for extended absences)

Attendance Standards

  • Excessive absenteeism or repeated tardiness will result in corrective action
  • No-call, no-show absences may result in immediate disciplinary action
  • Employees must obtain advance approval for planned time off

California law provides for various protected leave entitlements including Paid Sick Leave, FMLA/CFRA, and Pregnancy Disability Leave. Contact the Director for information regarding applicable leave rights.

13. PROGRESSIVE DISCIPLINE POLICY

Open Grace LLC utilizes a progressive discipline process when appropriate. The organization reserves the right to accelerate or skip steps based on the severity of conduct.

Corrective Action Steps

  1. Verbal Warning — documented discussion of the issue and expected correction
  2. Written Warning — formal written notice placed in the employee file
  3. Final Written Warning — notice that further violations may result in termination
  4. Suspension — temporary unpaid removal from duty pending review
  5. Termination — separation from employment
Certain conduct — including consumer abuse, falsification of records, confidentiality violations, or criminal activity — may result in immediate termination without prior warning steps.

14. GRIEVANCE & COMPLAINT PROCEDURE

Open Grace LLC encourages employees to raise workplace concerns promptly and in good faith.

Process

  1. Employee raises concern with their direct supervisor
  2. If unresolved or if the concern involves the supervisor, escalate to the Director
  3. The Director will conduct a review and respond within a reasonable timeframe
  4. External reporting options (California Labor Commissioner, DFEH, EEOC) remain available to all employees

Open Grace LLC strictly prohibits retaliation against any employee who raises a concern, files a complaint, or participates in an investigation in good faith.

15. TECHNOLOGY & DEVICE USE POLICY

Guidelines

  • Do not store confidential consumer information on personal devices without authorization
  • Do not photograph consumers or their homes without explicit written consent
  • Use of personal devices during direct client service should be limited to service-related purposes
  • Report any lost or stolen devices containing consumer information immediately
  • Organizational platforms (OneDrive, Staff Portal, MS Forms) must be used for all official documentation

16. HEALTH, SAFETY & EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

The safety of consumers, employees, and the public is a top priority at Open Grace LLC.

During a Home Visit Emergency

  1. Ensure the immediate safety of the consumer
  2. Call 911 if there is imminent danger, medical emergency, or threat of harm
  3. Contact the Director as soon as it is safe to do so
  4. Complete an incident report as soon as possible after the event

Injury or Illness

  • Report any work-related injury or illness to the Director immediately
  • Employees may be entitled to workers' compensation benefits under California law

Employees should never place themselves in a situation that poses an unreasonable risk to their own safety. If a visit environment is unsafe, leave and contact the Director immediately.
