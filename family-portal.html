What We Provide

CFS services we coordinate

Open Grace provides Coordinated Family Support through IRC Vendor PJ6208. Below are the core service areas we support based on your family's approved IPP goals.

🧭 Service Coordination Support

We work alongside your IRC Service Coordinator to keep communication clear, deadlines met, and your IPP goals on track. We act as a bridge — not a replacement.

🏡 Community Access & Inclusion

Supporting the individual in participating in community activities, classes, and social opportunities that build independence and connection.

👨‍👩‍👧 Family Training & Education

Skill-building sessions for family members to better understand developmental disabilities, advocacy, and how to support goals at home.

🧰 Independent Living Skills

Helping individuals develop practical life skills — budgeting, self-care, communication, transportation — based on their specific IPP goals.

📊 Progress Tracking & Reporting

Monthly check-ins, goal tracking, and quarterly reports ensure services are making a meaningful difference and documentation stays current.

🔗 Referral & Resource Navigation

When your needs go beyond CFS, we connect you to community programs, emergency resources, housing support, and other county services.

📋 Your services are guided by your IPP

Family Agreement

Your rights & acknowledgement

Before services begin, families receive and acknowledge key information about their rights, our responsibilities, and how we work together.

What you acknowledge when enrolling

At the start of services, Open Grace provides each family with written copies of the following. By signing your service agreement, you confirm you've received and understood:

  • Your rights as a CFS participant under the Lanterman Act and IRC policies
  • Open Grace's vendor responsibilities and service scope under your IPP
  • Privacy and confidentiality practices governing your family's PHI
  • Your right to file a grievance and the process for doing so
  • Emergency contact procedures and what to do in a crisis
  • How to request changes to services, goals, or frequency of contact

Need a copy of your signed agreement? Email us or call (951) 880-4132.

📜 Your rights under the Lanterman Act

As an IRC client, you have rights guaranteed by California's Lanterman Developmental Disabilities Services Act. These include the right to services that meet your needs, to participate in all planning decisions, and to have a qualified agency like Open Grace provide your services. Learn more at dds.ca.gov.

🔒 Privacy & Confidentiality

Your family's personal and health information is protected under HIPAA. Open Grace does not share your information with any third party without your written consent, except as required by law or IRC oversight.

✏️ Making Changes

You can request changes to your services, your assigned Navigator, or the frequency of contact at any time. Simply call our office or speak to your Navigator.

Call (951) 880-4132

🚨 Emergency Support

If your family faces an emergency related to CFS services, contact your Navigator first. For immediate safety crises, call 911. IRC after-hours: (800) 866-6223.

🔚 Ending Services

You or IRC may end CFS services at any time. Open Grace will provide 30 days' written notice if we need to discontinue services, giving your family time to transition smoothly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from families

If your question isn't answered here, call or text us at (951) 880-4132 — we're happy to help.

What is Coordinated Family Support (CFS)?

Coordinated Family Support is a service provided through California's regional center system that helps families caring for a person with a developmental disability. CFS provides coordination, training, and skill-building support to help the family unit — not just the individual — navigate services and build a better quality of life together.

How is Open Grace different from my IRC Service Coordinator?

Your IRC Service Coordinator manages your overall plan and authorizations. Open Grace is a CFS vendor — we provide the actual day-to-day support, coaching, and coordination services authorized in your IPP. We work alongside your SC, not instead of them.

Do we have to pay anything for CFS services?

No. CFS services authorized through IRC are funded by regional center and are provided at no cost to you. There are no copays, fees, or out-of-pocket costs for any Open Grace service under your approved IPP.

How often will we meet with our Navigator?

The frequency of contact depends on what's authorized in your IPP. Most families have at least one contact per month, often more during transitions or when working on active goals. Your Navigator will confirm the schedule at your first meeting.

Can we choose who our Navigator is?

We do our best to match families with a Navigator who fits their needs, including language preference. If you'd like to request a change after services begin, just speak with our Director Joshua Kennedy at (951) 880-4132.

What if we need services not currently in our IPP?

Open Grace can only provide services that are currently authorized in your Individual Program Plan. If you need something additional, we can help you document that need and work with your Service Coordinator to request a plan amendment.

Is my family's information kept private?

Yes. Your information is protected under HIPAA and California privacy law. We only share your information with IRC as required for billing and oversight. We will never share your family's information with outside parties without your written consent.

What languages do you provide services in?

We provide fully bilingual services in English and Spanish. All documents, our pre-screening form, and client communications are available in both languages. If you need services in another language, contact us and we will do our best to accommodate.

What happens if we're not happy with our services?

We take family satisfaction seriously. First, talk to your Navigator or our Director directly — most concerns are resolved quickly. If you're not satisfied, you have the right to file a formal grievance. See the for step-by-step instructions.

How do we stop services if we want to?

You may request to end CFS services with Open Grace at any time — simply contact us by phone or email. We'll coordinate with your IRC Service Coordinator to ensure a smooth transition. There is no penalty for ending services.

Your Rights

Grievance & complaint process

If you believe your rights have been violated or you're unsatisfied with Open Grace's services, you have the right to file a grievance. Here's how.

⚠️ Not getting the support you need right now?

Call or text our Director directly at (951) 880-4132 or email joshua.kennedy@opengrace.org. We respond to all service concerns within one business day.

1

Talk to your Navigator first

Many concerns can be resolved quickly at this level. Bring your concern to your assigned Navigator during your next contact, or call them directly. Ask for a resolution within 5 business days.

2

Contact the Open Grace Director

If your concern is not resolved with your Navigator, contact Director Joshua Kennedy at (951) 880-4132 or joshua.kennedy@opengrace.org. All director-level complaints receive a written response within 10 business days.

3

File a formal grievance with Open Grace

Request a Grievance Form by emailing info@opengrace.org or calling our office. We will acknowledge receipt within 3 business days and provide a written decision within 30 calendar days.

4

Contact Inland Regional Center

If you are not satisfied with Open Grace's resolution, contact IRC directly. Your Service Coordinator can initiate a vendor review, or contact IRC Quality Assurance at (909) 908-3600.

5

File with the Department of Developmental Services

California's DDS Office of Clients' Rights Advocacy (OCRA) handles complaints from regional center clients. Visit dds.ca.gov or call OCRA at (888) 860-4455.

📜 You will not face retaliation

Filing a grievance will never affect the services you receive from Open Grace. You have a legal right to raise concerns and file complaints, and we are required by IRC and DDS standards to protect that right.
