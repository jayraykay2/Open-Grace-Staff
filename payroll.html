Payroll & Timekeeping Policy

How to record your time, document your work, and get paid accurately and on schedule — in line with California labor law and Inland Regional Center documentation standards.

Applies to all Open Grace staff Effective July 2026 IRC Vendor PJ6208

Purpose

This policy exists so every employee is paid accurately and on time, and so the time we bill to Inland Regional Center is always backed by complete service documentation. You are responsible for recording all work hours accurately, completing your service documentation, and submitting time by the deadlines below.

Recording your time

Enter all work time in QuickBooks Time on the same day services are provided whenever possible. Every entry must include the consumer served, the service category, the accurate duration (quarter-hour increments: .25 / .50 / .75), and billable status where applicable.

One service record — the Daily Contact Log

Your detailed service notes belong in the Daily Contact Log in the Staff Portal, completed the same day as every client visit. The contact log is Open Grace's official service record. Your QuickBooks Time entry must match the corresponding contact log — same consumer, same date, same duration. Do not duplicate full narrative notes in QuickBooks Time.

  • Time must reflect actual hours worked — no rounding beyond the quarter-hour, no estimates.
  • No time may be logged for client-facing services by any staff member whose Live Scan clearance is still pending. Client contact is not permitted before clearance is confirmed.
  • Falsifying time records is grounds for disciplinary action up to and including termination.

Documentation

Time submitted for payroll must correspond with completed documentation: the same-day Daily Contact Log for every visit, any required forms, mileage documentation when applicable, and assigned follow-up documentation.

Your pay is protected. Hours you actually worked will be paid on the regular payday in accordance with California law — incomplete documentation will never delay payment of wages earned. Missing or late documentation is instead addressed as a performance matter through coaching and, if it continues, corrective action, and it may affect whether time can be billed to IRC.

Submission deadlines & supervisor review

Submit all time for the 1st–15th by 5:00 PM on the 15th, and all time for the 16th–end of month by 5:00 PM on the last calendar day.

Open Grace will make every effort to process late entries in the current payroll cycle, and all wages will be paid in compliance with California law. Repeatedly missing submission deadlines, however, may result in corrective action — the deadlines exist so your paycheck and our IRC billing both go out accurately and on time.

After each deadline, your supervisor reviews hours worked, cross-checks QuickBooks Time entries against Daily Contact Logs, and verifies service categories, consumer assignment, mileage, and billable status. You may be contacted to correct discrepancies before payroll is finalized.

Mileage

When travel is authorized, Open Grace reimburses business mileage at the current IRS standard mileage rate, consistent with California Labor Code §2802. Each mileage entry must include the date, starting location, destination, purpose of travel, and total business miles. Submit mileage with each payroll period.

Overtime

Obtain supervisor approval before working overtime. Under California law, non-exempt employees earn overtime at 1.5× the regular rate for hours over 8 in a workday or 40 in a workweek, and for the first 8 hours on a seventh consecutive workday — and 2× for hours over 12 in a workday or over 8 on that seventh day.

Unauthorized overtime must still be reported and will always be paid as the law requires; working overtime without approval may, however, result in corrective action.

Meal & rest breaks

  • Meal period: an unpaid, duty-free 30-minute meal period beginning before the end of your fifth hour of work (a second meal period before the end of the tenth hour on shifts over 10 hours).
  • Rest breaks: a paid 10-minute rest break for every 4 hours worked, or major fraction thereof.

Record meal periods accurately in QuickBooks Time. If your schedule ever prevents you from taking a required break, tell your supervisor the same day.

Paycheck review & corrections

Review each paycheck promptly. Please report any discrepancy within five business days so we can investigate and correct verified errors as quickly as possible — usually before the next payroll run.

Note: the five-day request helps us fix problems fast. It does not limit any right you have under California law to report or recover wages at any time.
