Who We Are

Open Grace LLC is a Coordinated Family Services (CFS) provider operating under contract with Inland Counties Regional Center (IRC). We support individuals and families navigating the regional center system by providing direct coordination, service navigation, and advocacy to ensure each client receives the care and support they are entitled to.

Your Role & Responsibilities

CFS Staff — core duties and expectations

✓ Read

CFS Staff — Core Responsibilities

  • Provide direct service coordination to assigned clients including referrals, appointments, and resource navigation under the guidance of your supervisor or director
  • Log all service contacts in the Open Grace CFS system within 24 hours of each interaction
  • Submit quarterly reports (QR) to IRC on time — by the 1st of the month following each 90-day interval
  • Maintain professional, consistent communication with clients, families, and IRC service coordinators
  • Monitor and manage authorized service hours — alert supervisor when approaching 80% of authorized limit
  • Attend all required staff meetings, trainings, and IRC compliance sessions
  • Maintain current CPR certification and Live Scan clearance at all times
  • Report all Special Incident Reports (SIRs) verbally within 24 hours and in writing within 48 hours

Documentation Requirement

All service contacts must be logged the same day or within 24 hours. Late or missing documentation affects IRC billing and quarterly reporting compliance.

Mandatory Reporting

Any incident involving client safety, abuse, neglect, or unusual circumstances must be reported to your supervisor immediately and documented as a Special Incident Report (SIR). There are no exceptions.

Client Interaction Protocols

How to work with clients, families, and IRC

✓ Read

First Contact with a New Client

1

Review the client file

Before any contact, review the Client Roster entry, IPP, and CDER in the CFS system. Note diagnosis, communication method, safety flags, and authorized services.

2

Introduce Open Grace

Contact the family via phone within 5 business days of assignment. Introduce yourself by name, explain your role, and confirm the best way to reach them going forward.

3

Assessment coordination

Assessment is conducted by supervisor or director. Your role is to coordinate logistics and communicate findings back to the family.

4

Obtain signed acknowledgments

Complete all three acknowledgment forms (Consent to Services, Client Rights, Emergency Contact) with the family. Use the acknowledgment forms on the staff portal.

Ongoing Client Contact

  • Maintain contact with each assigned client at minimum per the IRC-authorized service plan frequency
  • Log every interaction in the CFS system with date, duration, service type, and notes
  • Never promise services outside the client's authorized POS — coordinate with supervisor for any changes
  • Always communicate professionally — no personal relationships with clients or their families
  • If a client is unreachable for 2+ attempts, notify supervisor and document outreach attempts

Confidentiality

Client information is strictly confidential under W&I Code §4514. Do not discuss client matters in public spaces, via personal email or text, or with anyone not directly involved in the client's care.

Employment Requirements

Mandatory clearances and certifications

✓ Read

All requirements must be active before client contact

You may not be assigned to any client until Live Scan is cleared and CPR certification is current. This is a firm IRC requirement — no exceptions.

Required Clearances

Live Scan Background Check

  • Must be completed at an approved Live Scan site
  • Submit your Live Scan receipt to your supervisor immediately after completion
  • Clearance typically takes 3–10 business days after submission
  • Must be renewed if there is a break in service of 12+ months

CPR Certification

  • Must hold current CPR/AED certification from an approved provider (American Red Cross or American Heart Association)
  • Submit a copy of your certification card to your supervisor
  • Must be renewed before expiration — Open Grace will send a 60-day renewal reminder
  • Online-only CPR certifications are not accepted — must include an in-person skills component

Employment Eligibility — I-9

  • Federal law requires all employees to complete Form I-9 within 3 business days of their start date
  • You must present original, unexpired documents from List A, or one document each from List B and List C
  • Common documents: US Passport, Driver's License + Social Security Card, Driver's License + Birth Certificate
